Children are always the most vulnerable in an emergency and the scale of the catastrophe that struck Pakistan in August 2010 is simply overwhelming.

  • The monsoonal rains flooded more than 795,000 square kilometres of land, placing one fifth of the country under water.
  • Over 20 million people have been affected including nine million children.
  • Around two million homes are thought to have been damaged or destroyed across Pakistan and only a fraction of the displaced has been issued tents.
  • Over 10, 000 schools are partially or completely damaged, with over 1,900 schools being used as accommodation for 216,000 people.

Since the devasting floods, UNICEF has been on the ground providing humanitarian operations and aid. Amongst other responses, UNICEF has:

  • Provided safe drinking water to 4.5 million people daily;
  • Vaccinated 9 million children have been against measles and polio;
  • Provided over 1.5 million people with sanitation facilities;
  • Set up Temporary Learning Centres and restored education services to more than 100,000 children.

But nearly one year on and, despite the combined efforts of the world's largest charities, the situation is still desperate. Many people remain in need of life-saving humanitarian assistance and more funds are urgently needed. Please help us reach more vulnerable children and their families.
